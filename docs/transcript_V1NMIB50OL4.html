Asking Questions of Books


Transcript

Adler and Van Doren suggest that the first and most important rule of skillful reading, active reading, is asking questions and trying to answer them. And that really, if you just dwell on that and dwell on, well, what kinds of questions should I be asking and how should I go about asking them, how should I go about answering them when the author isn't present, and so on and so forth, then you'll get very far.

And they also say, conversely, and this isn't meant as a criticism, an undemanding reader asks no questions and gets no answers. And I certainly have read many, many books that way, particularly before I developed this habit. And I often found myself falling into that second category of the issue was not that I failed to remember things, but rather that my eyes just kind of skidded across paragraphs without even realizing it.
