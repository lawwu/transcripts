This is a whole history in the paper of false starts, wrong attempts, near misses, redefinitions of the problem. Ultimately, what Schieber offers as a better mode is the rational reconstruction approach. You don't present the actual history that you went through, but rather an idealized history that perfectly motivates each step in the solution. The goal in pursuing the rational reconstruction style is not to convince the reader that you're brilliant or addle-headed for that matter, but that your solution is trivial. It takes a certain strength of character to take that as one's goal.
